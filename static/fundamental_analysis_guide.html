Fundamental Analysis & Quant Forecasting Guide

A Comprehensive Guide to Stock Selection, Valuation Styles, and Quantitative Forecasting Models

1. Introduction to Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis is the process of evaluating a security's intrinsic value by examining related economic, financial, and other qualitative and quantitative factors. It is the cornerstone of intelligent capital allocation, aiming to separate high-quality assets from speculative market noise.

While traditional fundamental analysis asks: "Is this a good company with a durable competitive moat?", InvestIQ's Quantitative Analytics builds a bridge to statistics, asking: "What does the data say about the probability of this stock outperforming over the next period?"

Key Rule of Investing
"Never buy a business you do not understand. A stock is not just a ticker symbol; it represents fractional ownership in a real enterprise with revenues, costs, debts, and cash flows."

2. Selecting Stocks via Investment Styles

InvestIQ filters the stock universe based on six standard investment philosophies derived from academic financial research. Choose a style that matches your target duration and risk threshold:

  • Growth Investing: Focuses on companies with rapid earnings and top-line revenue growth (typically >15% CAGR). Investors look for expanding market share, high return on capital, and strong reinvestment opportunities.
  • Value Investing: Targets businesses trading below their intrinsic value. Key parameters include low Price-to-Earnings (P/E), low Price-to-Book (P/B), and positive free cash flows, seeking a margin of safety.
  • Quality Compounders: Focuses on businesses with exceptionally high returns on capital (ROCE >20%), near-zero debt, durable competitive advantages, and strong brand pricing power. Best held for long-term compound gains.
  • GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price): Blends Growth and Value styles by looking for companies with robust growth prospects but avoiding purchasing them at extreme valuations. The PEG ratio (<1.5) is the primary filter.
  • Dividend Investing: Focuses on stable dividend yields (>3%) backed by strong free cash flows and healthy payout ratios (30-70%), protecting investors from dividend traps paid via debt.
  • Turnaround Investing: Targets distressed companies undergoing operational restructuring (e.g. debt reduction, new management). High-risk but offers outsized recovery rewards if profitability turns positive.

4. InvestIQ Trend & Forecasting Engine

The InvestIQ Quant Engine builds a 12-module ensemble model based on a stock's 365-day historical OHLCV data. Below is how the engine calculates signals and predictions:

  • Modules 1 & 5: Trend & Strength (ADX): Analyzes order of 20, 50, 100, and 200 DMAs. If 20 > 50 > 100 > 200, a Strong Uptrend is declared. Trend strength is measured via ADX; values above 25 confirm a strong trending regime.
  • Module 2: Momentum Analysis: Calculates price returns over 7, 30, 90, 180, and 365 trading days, measuring Relative Strength against benchmark indices (Nifty 50) and sector peers.
  • Modules 3 & 4: Volatility & Support/Resistance: Computes historical price volatility and Average True Range (ATR). Detects structural support (price floor) and resistance (price ceiling) zones based on volume concentration.
  • Module 6: Volume Confirmation: Checks price-volume correlation. Higher volume on up-days confirms "Accumulation" (institutional buying). Higher volume on down-days indicates "Distribution".
  • Modules 7 & 8: Ensemble Forecast & Confidence: Combines Prophet (seasonal components), XGBoost (lag features), LSTM (neural returns model), and Linear Regression. The engine runs a 30-day historical validation backtest to measure MAPE and MAE, displaying the forecast accuracy.
  • Modules 9 - 12: Interpretation & Trading Signals: Analyzes expected move direction and compile signals (STRONG BUY, BUY, HOLD, REDUCE, or SELL) based on weighted scores: Trend (30%), Momentum (20%), Forecast Return (25%), Volume (15%), and Volatility (10%).
